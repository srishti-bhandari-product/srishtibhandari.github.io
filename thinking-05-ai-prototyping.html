AI product prototyping, a quick review

I tested Lovable, UX Pilot AI, and Google Stitch to see how quickly a PM can move from product idea to visual prototype.

Product managers often need to make an idea visible before the team is ready to fully design or build it. A clear prototype can accelerate alignment, expose weak assumptions, and help stakeholders react to something concrete.

I tested three tools that promise to shorten that distance: Lovable, UX Pilot AI, and Google Stitch.

Lovable

Lovable is the most immediately polished of the three. It turns a product concept into usable screens quickly, which makes it useful when the goal is alignment rather than perfect fidelity.

  • Turns product concepts into clean, usable screens.
  • Works well for fast iteration.
  • Best for early alignment and stakeholder buy-in.

UX Pilot AI

UX Pilot AI starts closer to the customer journey. It is less about jumping straight into screens and more about shaping the flow around user goals.

  • Starts with user goals before moving into UI.
  • Makes flows feel more intentional.
  • Best for CX-led teams that think in journeys first.

Google Stitch

Stitch still feels early, but it is interesting if your team already works heavily in the Google and Figma ecosystem. I would treat it more as a mockup accelerator than a complete prototyping system today.

  • Better for mockups than full prototypes right now.
  • Evolves with the product brief.
  • Integrates smoothly into Figma.

The takeaway

These tools do not replace designers. They give PMs a faster way to make early thinking visible.

The real value is not the screen itself. It is the speed at which a team can react, refine, and decide whether an idea is worth taking further.
