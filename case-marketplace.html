Case Study

Building a Trusted Resale Experience at Enterprise Scale

Public-safe overview focused on customer experience: clarity, value transparency, and trust design.

Resale program visual anchor (public-facing).

Context

Resale and refurbished commerce continues to grow rapidly, driven by value-conscious customers and evolving purchasing behavior. Non-new inventory existed across multiple categories, but customer engagement was limited. Most shoppers did not intentionally search for resale items, and trust concerns suppressed conversion when they did encounter them.

The opportunity was to design a resale experience that felt clear, fair, and trustworthy — without fragmenting the core shopping journey.

The Challenge

Three core customer barriers emerged:

  • Discoverability: Customers rarely searched for non-new items directly, and resale offers were often hidden behind secondary interactions.
  • Clarity: Condition differences were difficult to compare, making it harder for customers to evaluate value versus new.
  • Confidence: Customers expressed hesitation around item condition accuracy, seller reliability cues, and “what happens if something goes wrong.”

Addressing these required coordinated changes across Search, Item Page, pricing presentation, and trust signals — not isolated UI updates.

My Role

As Principal Product Manager, I led strategy and execution across the resale experience, partnering with Search, Personalization, Pricing, and Design teams to:

  • Increase visibility of non-new offers within existing shopping flows
  • Improve condition transparency and customer education
  • Reframe value through cross-condition price comparisons
  • Ensure fairness and consistency in how offers were surfaced
  • Establish a scalable trust framework for high-consideration categories

The goal was to make resale feel integrated — not secondary.

Optimizing Offer Presentation Across Conditions

Historically, new-condition offers were prioritized by default, even when non-new alternatives provided meaningful value. We redesigned offer presentation to evaluate and surface the most relevant offer across conditions, introduce clear savings callouts when appropriate, and maintain guardrails that preserve a consistent experience.

Result: Improved engagement and conversion across resale inventory while maintaining overall marketplace health.

Improving Discovery Without Increasing Friction

Customers rarely began their journey searching for non-new items. Instead of expecting intent to change, we embedded resale visibility into existing behavior through earlier surfacing of non-new options, clear entry points to explore alternate conditions, and more intuitive exposure of savings versus new.

Result: Greater traffic to non-new offers and stronger click-through from core search journeys.

Designing for Trust and Psychological Safety

Voice-of-customer insights revealed that resale hesitation was primarily emotional — not economic. Customers asked:

  • “Is this condition accurate?”
  • “Can I trust what I’m buying?”
  • “What happens if something is wrong?”

We redesigned key components to surface condition-specific information more clearly, introduce richer credibility cues, highlight protections more transparently, and standardize condition terminology to reduce ambiguity.

Result: Higher engagement with condition education and measurable lift in add-to-cart behavior for non-new items.

What Changed

  • Before: Resale was discoverable but secondary; condition differences were unclear; trust cues were implicit.
  • After: Resale was integrated into primary shopping flows; value comparison was transparent; trust signals were structured and intentional.

Resale evolved from a hidden assortment to a credible, value-forward extension of the marketplace.
